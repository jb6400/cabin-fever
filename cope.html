Cabin Fever

The coping mechanism that works best for you will have a lot to do with your personal situation. Finding meaningful ways to engage your brain and occupy your time can help alleviate the distress and irritability that cabin fever brings.

These tips might help relieve some of your pent-up cabin fever:

Connect with nature

Invest in some houseplants. Houseplants can help a space feel more lively and connected to the outside world.

Get some ambient noise going.

Stick with a routine

Add these healthy habits to your regular schedule:

  • Eat nutritious meals
  • Exercise
  • Protect your slumber
    •

    Try something new

    Learn a new skill: Sign up for an online art class, learn to speak Mandarin, try baking French macarons or teach yourself a chord on the guitar you bought seven years ago and never learned to play.

    Connect

    In the age of social distancing, use virtual connections to fill the gap:

  • Schedule a happy hour: Plan a regular time to video chat with your friends or family members
  • Write letters: Texts are practically essential during self-isolation, but it’s fun to channel your inner Victorian.
  • Open up: When talking with your loved ones ­­­­(in person or virtually),­ you don’t have to put on a brave face. “Being honest about your worries, fears and future dreams can bring you closer and make your relationship stronger,” Dr. Bea says.

    Find moments of zen

    Find soothing ways to peace out:Take up meditation

    Get some alone time: If you’re at home with other people, the constant togetherness might be making you extra antsy..

    Use your brain

    solving a problem or meeting a professional challenge can give you a sense of accomplishment and satisfaction:

  • Do something you’ve been putting off: There’s no time like the present to tackle a project you’ve been putting off because it’s too challenging or time-consuming. “Start your novel, make albums of your family photos or organize your disaster of a basement,”
  • Read a book
  • Redecorate and/or rearrange your space
