Cabin Fever

Cabin fever is a series of negative emotions and distressing sensations people may face if theyâ€™re isolated or feeling cut off from the world.

These feelings of loneliness are more likely in times of social distancing, self-quarantining during a pandemic, or sheltering in place.

The origin of the term dates back to the early 1900s in North America, when it may have referred to someone who was isolated in a remote area, or cabin, especially during the winter when it was necessary to stay indoors for days at a time. Another explanation traces further back to the early 1800s, when the phrase might have referred to being home bound with typhus fever.
